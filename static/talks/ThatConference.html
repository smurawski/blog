DevOps at Stack Exchange

Defining DevOps - DevOps

DevOps is a term that was coined by Patrick Debois

DevOps describes the framework of breaking communications silos and encouraging inter-disciplinary cooperation and integration.

DevOps acknowledges that problems in software/IT projects is not the responsibility of Devs or IT alone.

DevOps can be thought of as the convergence of three specific movements:

  • Agile Infrastructure - Infrastructure as Code (Debois/Shafer)
  • Velocity - Starting with John Allspaw's 10 Deploys a Day
  • Lean Startup - Minimum Viable Product (Ries) and Continuous Delivery (Humble)

DevOps is about CAMS - Culture, Automation, Measurement, and Sharing.

The Three Ways

The First Way - Systems Thinking

Avoid local optima - strive to improve the whole process.

Second Way - Amplify Feeback Loops

Get the results back in the hands of those who can use them. Quickly!

Third Way - Culture of Continual Experimentation and Learning

Be willing to change things, and keep changing.

It's all about the Culture.

The key to a successful DevOps environment is culture.

One major component to that is the reducing the barriers to communication.

Improving communication between teams is critical.

Fortunately, technology helps make this easy.

It's people that make this difficult.

We use a variety of tools to help with communication. In no particular order:

  • Email
  • Google Hangouts
  • Vidyo Video Conferencing
  • Trello Boards
  • Chat
  • Skype
  • Google Talk

OUTAGE!

When you think of an outage in a traditional IT environment, this is what comes to mind.

Someone, usually upper management, is calling for the head of whomever is responsible.

People scramble, not only to resolve the outage, but to make sure their backsides are covered.

Not only does this impede recovery time, but limits the organizations ability to learn from the outage.

Since there isn't an incentive to show culpablity and there are major disincentives to acknowledging it, getting to the true root cause can be rough.

So, what happens at Stack Exchange?

At Stack Exchange, blame is used only to find the root of a problem.

We've even made it somewhat of a game...

Since there isn't a stigma to blame, people are willing to disclose changes they've made or actions they've taken.

This allows you to get to root cause that much faster.

And focus on resolving the problem, rather than worrying about our job.

Increasing Visibility

  • Stack Exchange Status
  • External Monitoring
  • Weekly Status Reports

  • Stack Exchange Build
  • Source Control - Code and Configuration

You may have noticed that I included configuration with source code.

This is not an accident.

Our server configurations are versioned and managed like applications.

By treating our servers this way, we can recreate our environment

physically, virtually, or on some cloud provider with minimal modification.

All the members of our technical staff have access to our build server(s).

Whom ever need to can build, but if you build, be prepared to deal with the consequences.
